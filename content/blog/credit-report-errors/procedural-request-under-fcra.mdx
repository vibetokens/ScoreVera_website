After a dispute is verified, most consumers don't know they have the right to ask the bureau to document exactly how it conducted its investigation. A procedural request under FCRA § 611(a)(7) compels the bureau to explain the method of verification — which often reveals that the investigation was far less rigorous than the law requires.

## What Is a Procedural Request?

A procedural request is a written demand, submitted within 15 days of receiving a dispute verification notice, asking the credit bureau to disclose what business records were reviewed, what procedures were followed, and who was contacted during the investigation. Under § 611(a)(7), the bureau must provide you with a description of the procedure used if you make this request within the required timeframe.

## When to File a Procedural Request

File after any dispute result where the item was verified but you believe the investigation was superficial. If the bureau verified the item simply by sending an E-OSCAR code to the furnisher and receiving a confirmation back, the description of procedures will reveal exactly that — giving you grounds to argue that no "reasonable reinvestigation" occurred.

## How to Write the Request

Your letter should reference the original dispute date, the date of the bureau's verification response, and FCRA § 611(a)(7). State that you are requesting, within the 15-day statutory window, a full description of the procedures used to verify the disputed information, including what documents were reviewed and what parties were contacted.

## What to Do With the Response

If the bureau's procedural description shows they did nothing more than ask the furnisher and accept a coded response, you have the evidence you need to build an escalation. Include this response in a CFPB complaint. It is also key evidence for a consumer rights attorney evaluating whether the bureau met its legal standard.

## The 15-Day Deadline Is Strict

Unlike the 30-day dispute window, the 15-day window for a procedural request starts from when you receive the verification notice. Track this carefully — missing the window means losing this specific right.
