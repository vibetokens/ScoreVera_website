## Wyoming Statute of Limitations on Debt

Wyoming has a longer-than-average statute of limitations for consumer debt. Open accounts like credit cards and medical bills carry an eight-year window. Formal written contracts like personal loans and promissory notes have a ten-year limitation period.

| Debt Type | Statute of Limitations |
|---|---|
| **Credit card debt (open account)** | 8 years |
| **Medical debt** | 8 years |
| **Auto loans (written contract)** | 10 years |
| **Personal loans (written contract)** | 10 years |
| **Oral contracts** | 8 years |
| **Promissory notes** | 10 years |

These are among the longest limitation periods in the country. A signed personal loan from over nine years ago may still be legally actionable in Wyoming. The SOL runs from the date of default or last payment.

**Critical note:** Never assume a debt is time-barred in Wyoming without confirming the exact default date against these windows. Partial payment or acknowledgment can also restart the clock.

## Wyoming-Specific Consumer Protections

**Wyoming Consumer Protection Act (Wyo. Stat. § 40-12-101 et seq.)**
Wyoming's Consumer Protection Act prohibits deceptive trade practices. The AG has primary enforcement authority. Consumers may also bring private lawsuits in some circumstances, though Wyoming's private enforcement provisions are narrower than many states. Civil penalties and injunctive relief are available through AG action.

**Wyoming Division of Banking**
Wyoming regulates financial institutions and some debt collectors through the Division of Banking. Certain entities conducting collection activity in Wyoming must comply with state licensing requirements.

**Wyoming Homestead Exemption**
Wyoming provides a homestead exemption of up to $20,000 in equity on a primary residence — more modest than some states, but still provides meaningful protection against creditor seizure post-judgment.

**Wage Garnishment**
Wyoming follows federal garnishment limits: the lesser of 25% of disposable earnings or amounts exceeding 30 times the federal minimum wage per week.

## Federal Rights That Apply Regardless of State

- **Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA):** Dispute inaccurate items; bureaus must investigate within 30 days.
- **Fair Debt Collection Practices Act (FDCPA):** Federal collector conduct standards.
- **Fair Credit Billing Act (FCBA):** Billing dispute protections on credit card accounts.

File at consumerfinance.gov/complaint.

## Your First Action Step

Wyoming's long limitation periods mean collectors have substantial time to pursue legal action. Don't rely on the assumption that an old debt is expired — verify the exact default date against the eight- or ten-year window. Pull your credit reports at annualcreditreport.com, document the original delinquency date on every collection account, and dispute any inaccurate information in writing by certified mail to each bureau.
