## Virginia Statute of Limitations on Debt

Virginia distinguishes between written and oral contracts. Most consumer debt backed by written documentation — credit cards, medical bills, auto loans — gives collectors five years to sue. Undocumented oral agreements expire in three years.

| Debt Type | Statute of Limitations |
|---|---|
| **Credit card debt (open account)** | 5 years |
| **Medical debt** | 5 years |
| **Auto loans (written contract)** | 5 years |
| **Personal loans (written contract)** | 5 years |
| **Oral contracts** | 3 years |
| **Promissory notes** | 5 years |

The SOL runs from the date of default or last payment. After the applicable period expires, collectors cannot obtain a judgment in Virginia court. You must raise the SOL as an affirmative defense if sued.

**Note:** Virginia courts have found that partial payment or written acknowledgment of a debt can restart the SOL. Handle old accounts carefully before taking any action.

## Virginia-Specific Consumer Protections

**Virginia Consumer Protection Act (VCPA, Va. Code § 59.1-196 et seq.)**
Virginia's Consumer Protection Act prohibits fraudulent acts, deceptive practices, and false advertising in consumer transactions. The AG and private consumers can bring claims. Successful plaintiffs recover actual damages; courts can award civil penalties up to $2,500 per willful violation plus attorney's fees.

**Virginia Debt Collection Act (Va. Code § 18.2-213)**
Virginia has specific criminal and civil prohibitions on debt collection fraud and harassment. While enforcement is primarily through the AG, these provisions supplement consumer remedies.

**Virginia Consumer Data Protection Act (VCDPA)**
Virginia enacted comprehensive data privacy legislation in 2021. While primarily a data privacy law, it gives consumers rights to access and correct personal data held by businesses — rights that can intersect with credit reporting disputes.

**Virginia Wage Garnishment**
Virginia follows federal garnishment limits: 25% of disposable earnings or amounts exceeding 30 times the federal minimum wage per week, whichever is less. Virginia exempts certain income types from garnishment.

## Federal Rights That Apply Regardless of State

- **Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA):** Dispute inaccurate items; bureaus must respond within 30 days.
- **Fair Debt Collection Practices Act (FDCPA):** Federal collector conduct standards.
- **Fair Credit Billing Act (FCBA):** Billing error protections.

File at consumerfinance.gov/complaint.

## Your First Action Step

Pull your credit reports at annualcreditreport.com. Any written contract debt with a default date more than five years ago is time-barred for litigation in Virginia. Identify every collection account, verify the original delinquency date, and dispute any inaccurate information in writing by certified mail to each bureau. If you're facing a lawsuit on a potentially time-barred debt, file a response raising the SOL defense immediately.
