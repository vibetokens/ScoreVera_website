## West Virginia Statute of Limitations on Debt

West Virginia has one of the most creditor-favorable SOL structures in the US: ten years for written contracts and five years for oral agreements. This means collectors retain the ability to sue on written debts for a full decade.

| Debt Type | Statute of Limitations |
|---|---|
| **Credit card debt (open account)** | 10 years |
| **Medical debt** | 10 years |
| **Auto loans (written contract)** | 10 years |
| **Personal loans (written contract)** | 10 years |
| **Oral contracts** | 5 years |
| **Promissory notes** | 10 years |

The ten-year SOL is comparable only to Rhode Island and some categories in Illinois, Missouri, and Kentucky. Do not assume any consumer debt is time-barred in West Virginia without verifying the exact default date is more than ten years ago.

The clock runs from the date of default or last payment. After expiration, lawsuits are time-barred — but in West Virginia, that expiration is a long time coming.

## West Virginia-Specific Consumer Protections

**West Virginia Consumer Credit and Protection Act (WVCCPA, W. Va. Code § 46A-1-101 et seq.)**
West Virginia's WVCCPA is a comprehensive consumer protection statute with real teeth. It covers unfair or deceptive acts in credit transactions and debt collection. Consumers can bring private lawsuits and recover actual damages plus $1,000 per violation (for willful violations of the debt collection provisions), plus attorney's fees. The Act covers original creditors as well as third-party debt collectors — a significant protection beyond the federal FDCPA.

**West Virginia Attorney General Enforcement**
The AG has authority to investigate and pursue violators under the WVCCPA and can seek injunctive relief, civil penalties, and restitution for consumers.

**Wage Garnishment in West Virginia**
West Virginia follows federal garnishment limits: the lesser of 25% of disposable earnings or amounts exceeding 30 times the federal minimum wage per week. Certain income types — Social Security, veterans' benefits — are exempt from garnishment.

- Online complaint form available

**West Virginia Division of Financial Institutions**
- Website: dfi.wv.gov
- Phone: (304) 558-2294
- For complaints about regulated financial entities

## Federal Rights That Apply Regardless of State

- **Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA):** Dispute inaccurate items; bureaus have 30 days to investigate.
- **Fair Debt Collection Practices Act (FDCPA):** Federal collector standards, supplemented by WVCCPA.
- **Fair Credit Billing Act (FCBA):** Billing error protections.

File at consumerfinance.gov/complaint.

## Your First Action Step

West Virginia's ten-year SOL means most debt from the last decade is still legally actionable. Don't assume anything is expired without confirming the exact default date. Pull your credit reports at annualcreditreport.com, document the original delinquency date for every collection account, and dispute any inaccurate information in writing by certified mail to each bureau. If a collector is using deceptive tactics, the WVCCPA's $1,000 per-violation damages provision gives you a meaningful remedy.
