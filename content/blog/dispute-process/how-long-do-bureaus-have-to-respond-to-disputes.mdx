One of the most powerful consumer protections in the Fair Credit Reporting Act is its mandatory response timeline. Credit bureaus cannot ignore your dispute — they are legally required to investigate and respond within a defined window.

Knowing these deadlines puts you in a stronger position to escalate when a bureau doesn't follow the law.

## The Standard 30-Day Rule

Under FCRA § 611(a)(1), a credit bureau must complete its investigation within 30 days of receiving your dispute. This applies to Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion equally. The clock starts on the day they receive your dispute — not the day you mailed it.

## The 45-Day Extension

If you provide additional information to the bureau after your initial dispute is submitted, the investigation window extends to 45 days. This extension only applies when you're the one submitting new evidence — it doesn't give the bureau extra time on its own.

## The 5-Day Notification Rule

After completing its investigation, the bureau has 5 business days to send you the written results. These results must include: what was investigated, whether items were modified or deleted, and a notice of your right to add a statement of dispute to your file.

## What Happens When Bureaus Miss the Deadline

If a bureau fails to respond within the required timeframe, the disputed item must be deleted. You should document your timeline carefully — note the certified mail delivery date and calculate the 30-day window from there. A missed deadline is also grounds for a CFPB complaint.

## Practical Tips for Tracking Responses

Keep a dedicated folder for each dispute. Log the delivery confirmation date, set a calendar reminder for day 30, and save all written responses from the bureau. If you don't hear back, send a follow-up letter referencing the original dispute date and the FCRA deadline.
